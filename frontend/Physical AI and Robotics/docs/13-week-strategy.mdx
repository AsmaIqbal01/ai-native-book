# 13-Week Learning Strategy: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Course Overview

This 13-week course provides a comprehensive introduction to Physical AI and humanoid robotics, covering the fundamental concepts, technologies, and practical implementations needed to understand and work with modern robotic systems. The course follows a progressive learning approach, building from foundational concepts to advanced multimodal AI systems.

## Prerequisites

- Basic programming knowledge (Python preferred)
- Understanding of linear algebra and calculus basics
- Familiarity with Linux command line
- Interest in robotics and AI technologies

## Weekly Breakdown

### Week 1: Introduction to Physical AI
**Duration**: 7 days (Day 1-7)

**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand the concept of Physical AI vs. traditional digital AI
- Explore the landscape of humanoid robotics platforms (Tesla Optimus, Figure AI, Boston Dynamics, etc.)
- Learn about the importance of embodiment in AI systems
- Study physical laws and their application in robotics

**Content Distribution**:
- Day 1-2: What is Physical AI? (Theory + Examples)
- Day 3-4: Embodied Intelligence concepts
- Day 5-6: Physical laws in robotics
- Day 7: Review and practical exercises

**Key Concepts**:
- Embodiment and its importance in AI systems
- Differences between digital AI and embodied intelligence
- Physical constraints in robotic systems
- Sensor systems in humanoid robots

**Resources**:
- Chapter 1 readings
- Video lectures on Physical AI concepts
- Research papers on embodied intelligence

**Self-Assessment**:
- Quiz on Physical AI concepts
- Discussion forum participation
- Reflection essay on Physical vs. Digital AI

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this 13-week course, students will be able to:

1. Design and implement Physical AI systems with multimodal capabilities
2. Integrate ROS 2 with NVIDIA Isaac for advanced robotics applications
3. Create digital twins for robot development and testing
4. Implement Vision-Language-Action systems for natural human-robot interaction
5. Apply reinforcement learning techniques to robotic problems
6. Ensure safety and ethical considerations in Physical AI systems
7. Evaluate and optimize robot systems for real-world deployment

Before starting Week 1, students should be able to:
- Write basic Python scripts
- Navigate Linux command line

- Have access to appropriate hardware or cloud computing resources
